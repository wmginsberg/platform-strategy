How can I build a website?

People are always asking what the best way to get involved with coding is. The short (and long) answer is that there are about 1 bajillion ways to get started, so the advice I give is the advice I get from developer friends when I want to learn something new in development as well: pick a project and learn by building it.

Static websites are cool, dynamic web apps are even cooler. Both can be a daunting project to take on even if you have a bit of coding experience, when the former is actually a pretty simple thing to make!

To help you get past staring at a blank Sublime file, the web development equivalent of staring at a blank Word (or Google) doc when writing a paper, I've written up this quiz.

Note: I didn't build this web app myself, I stole it (with permission) from Owen.
Other Note: This is my humble advice, it\'s certainly not Google's opinion or 100% (or even 50%) comprehensive. If you have suggestions/additions feel free to submit a PR on GitHub, or file your complaints as Issues!

What is your development experience?

1. Do you have a Computer Science (or related) degree?
2. Have you taken 1 or 2 Computer Science classes?
3. Have you taken 3 or more Computer Science classes
4. Have you built an iOS or Android app?
5. Have you worked with Wordpress or Squarespace?
6. Do you know HTML?
7. Do you know R or Matlab?
8. Do you know SQL?
9. Do you know Python?
10. Do you know JavaScript?
11. Have you worked with the Terminal (/shell/command line)?
12. Have you used Markdown or LaTeX?
13. Have you ever programmed in any capacity before?

What is your goal?

14. To see what programming is all about.
15. To build a static website for fun.
16. To build a personal or portfolio website.
17. To build a game or service.
18. To build an online shop.

What is your budget?

19. Free (<$1 per month).
20. Low (~$10 per month)
21. Mid (~$25 per month)
22. High (~$100 per month)
